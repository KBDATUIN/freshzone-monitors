Air Quality Guide: PM Sensors & AQI

Live Sensor Education
PM1.0Tiny particles from fresh smoke/aerosol. They can go deep into lungs and spread quickly in enclosed rooms.
PM2.5Fine particles used for AQI calculation. Higher PM2.5 means higher short-term respiratory irritation risk.
PM10Larger inhalable dust/smoke particles. Useful for understanding overall particulate load in the area.

How sensors work

The node uses laser scattering. More airborne particles reflect more light, so FreshZone estimates PM concentration in µg/m³ every few seconds.

How AQI works here

AQI converts PM2.5 into a health index. It gives a quick risk level so staff can react faster without reading raw PM values first.

Why three PM values

PM1.0 catches very fine aerosol, PM2.5 powers alerts and AQI, PM10 adds larger-particle context for ventilation and cleanup decisions.
